I'm a Junior Research Scientist in the Gender Equity and Demographic Research Project
at the International Institute for Population Sciences, Mumbai.

I earned my PhD in Biostatistics & Demography from the International Institute for Population Sciences, Mumbai.

My research primarily focuses on the empirical analysis of population health and well-being using standard and advanced statistical and epidemiological methods. More explicitly, my research interest includes studying the dynamics of gender inequality due to household/family hierarchy, community effects on health and mortality, trends and risk factors of health and mortality, and transition-specific risk factors of co-morbid diseases.

I envision myself being a proficient scholar, conducting studies and analyses to provide answers to a variety of real-world problems.
